How To Use This Page

  1. Read one lesson block and focus only on that letter group.
  2. Open Studio and practice those letters until you can answer without hints.
  3. Use Freehand Quiz to write short words containing the same letters.
  4. Check the cheat sheet whenever forms blur together.

Lesson 1

Core Vowels

ᠠ ᠡ ᠢ ᠣ ᠤ

  • These are your highest-value starter letters.
  • Focus especially on the O/U pair: ᠣ vs ᠤ.
  • When reading a word, identifying the first vowel usually gives the biggest clue.
Lesson 2

Front And Extended Vowels

ᠥ ᠦ ᠧ

  • Master ᠥ vs ᠦ as a pair, just like ᠣ vs ᠤ.
  • ASCII-friendly typing often uses oe and ue.
  • ᠧ is useful for fuller chart coverage and borrowed spellings.
Lesson 3

Nasals And Liquids

ᠨ ᠩ ᠮ ᠯ ᠷ

  • Spend extra time on the N/NG contrast: ᠨ vs ᠩ.
  • Practice these inside short syllable chunks, not in isolation only.
  • These letters appear frequently in real words and endings.
Lesson 4

Core Stops And Fricatives

ᠪ ᠫ ᠬ ᠭ ᠰ ᠱ ᠲ ᠳ

  • Watch the close pair ᠲ vs ᠳ.
  • Use vowel class clues to handle ᠬ and ᠭ more confidently.
  • Train similar letters side by side before speed practice.
Lesson 5

Affricates And Glides

ᠴ ᠵ ᠶ ᠸ

  • Lock in ᠴ vs ᠵ early to reduce reading mistakes later.
  • ᠶ and ᠸ become easier when seen inside converted words.
  • This lesson rounds out most everyday core chart recognition.
Lesson 6

Extended And Loan Letters

ᠹ ᠺ ᠻ ᠼ ᠽ ᠾ

  • These letters are common in names and borrowed vocabulary.
  • Prioritize recognition first, then production speed.
  • Keep reviewing core letters so this lesson does not crowd out basics.
Lesson 7

Rare Chart Letters (Reference Level)

ᠿ ᡀ ᡁ ᡂ

  • These are low-frequency for beginners but useful for complete chart knowledge.
  • Treat them as recognition targets unless your curriculum requires deeper use.
  • Knowing them helps you avoid confusion in full-script reference tables.

Main Grammar Notes (Easy Language)

  • Basic order is often Subject-Object-Verb, so verbs are often near the end.
  • Mongolian uses many suffixes to add grammar meaning.
  • Suffix vowels usually follow vowel harmony (back vs front class).
  • Postpositions typically come after the noun phrase.

Milestone Checklist

  • I can identify major vowels and consonants without hint text.
  • I know high-value confusion pairs and how to resolve them.
  • I can transliterate a short word and write it by typing or freehand.
  • I can explain basic vowel harmony in simple terms.
